FAQ

What is Old School Bingo?

Old School Bingo is a side project born out of the realization of the considerable effort involved in organizing a clan bingo. My goal was to streamline the process by automating cumbersome tasks, and in doing so, I introduced additional features that would otherwise be laborious to incorporate.

How do i create a bingo?

To create a bingo, navigate to the designated page where you'll be prompted to input details. Once submitted, you'll be redirected to the edit page. Here, you can tailor individual tiles, manage teams and players, fine-tune bingo settings, and incorporate Discord notifications to enhance your bingo experience.

How do i join a bingo?

To join a bingo, simply explore public bingos or utilize the invitation link provided by the bingo moderator. On the bingo's homepage, enter your Old School RuneScape account name if required, and then choose to join an existing team or create a new one.

How do i play in a bingo?

After joining a bingo, once it commences, locate the link to your board on the bingo's homepage. Click on any tile to access its description and submit images accordingly. If the bingo is public, you can choose other teams' boards or view a summary from the dropdown menu on the right side of your board page.

Is Old School Bingo free?

Absolutely, running a bingo in Old School Bingo is open source and entirely free. Our aim is to provide essential features without any cost, ensuring accessibility for everyone.

What are the future plans for Old School Bingo?

Upon achieving a seamless user experience and ensuring a bug-free product, the future plans for Old School Bingo involve the gradual rollout of new features based on user feedback. Key upcoming initiatives include the introduction of tile packs to simplify image design for each tile, completing the Runelite plugin for direct in-game tracking, and unveiling a fresh style of bingo.

I have a question or would like to help?

If you have any questions or would like to contribute, feel free to join the Discord server. The website is open source, and everyone is welcome to contribute. As I'm relatively new to Discord servers and open-source projects, there might be some features missing, but I am open to suggestions and feedback.
